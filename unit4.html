Wave Effects of Light

When looking at oil spills, blowing bubbles, or angling a CD towards the light, we can see the spectrum on these objects. This occurs because light is being reflected by or transmitted through a thin film, which causes an optical interference, allowing us to see the colours of the rainbow.
