Dynamics


Dynamics; the word can have many meanings. Is it the varying levels of volume in a musical piece that give it interest? Or is it the properties that stimulate growth or development within a group of people? Or, is it the branch of mechanics in which bodies move according to the forces which act upon it? Ding ding! The correct answer is all 3! But in this case, we will be talking more about the 3rd definition because it applies to physics.

As stated before, dynamics in physics is the branch of mechanics that deals with the effect that forces have on an object's motion. These principals were founded by an english mathematician and physicist named Isaac Newton. You may have heard of the story of Sir Isaac Newton sitting under an apple tree, when the fruit suddenly fell over his head. This simple movement is actually what piqued his curiosity and led to him studying the principals of gravity and creating the 3 laws of motion that we know today. Newton’s laws explain how every single object in our daily lives move.

Newton's 1st Law

Also known as “the law of inertia”, this law states thatif the external net force acting on an object is zero, the object will remain at rest or at constant velocity.

Newton's 2nd Law

Force = mass x acceleration,summarized by the equation F = ma. This law states that we can use this equation to do calculations regarding the force, mass, and acceleration of an object.

Newton's 3rd Law

For every action force, a simultaneous reaction force will occur that is equal in magnitude but opposite in direction.

Real-Life Applications


  • 1st Law: Newton’s first law can be seen in the example of a moving cart. The cart will continue moving at the same velocity unless friction, for instance, is acted upon it, causing it to decelerate and come to a stop. Another example is a rock at rest. The rock will continue to stay put if no external forces act on it.

  • 2nd Law: Here is an example for Newton’s second law. If you take a look to the right at the gif, you see a girl riding a bicycle. In this case, the bicycle is the mass, and the girls legs are the force acting on the pedals to push it forward. If she were to increase the amount of force applied on the pedals, the bicycle’s speed would accelerate.

  • 3rd Law: Newton’s third law can be seen all around us, specifically in the way people move in their daily lives! Taking a look at this gif, it is of a man walking as normal. As he walks across the floor, he is exerting a force against the floor. At the same time, the floor is exerting a force against him. The forces being exerted here are equal in magnitude, but opposite in direction!
