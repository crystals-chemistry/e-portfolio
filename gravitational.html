Orbits

A satellite is an object that revolves around another body due to gravitational attraction. For example, the moon is a natural satellite that revolves around Earth. Artificial satellites are human-made objects that have been placed into orbit around Earth or around other planets or bodies in our solar system.

The international space station (ISS) is a permanent orbiting laboratory that is used to conduct many different research projects. Scientists also use the ISS to study human responses to microgravity (10-6 times the value of g). The value of g at the altitude of the ISS is approximately 8.7 m/s2. Microgravity is present when an object is in free fall, which astronauts on the ISS experience as they are in a constant state of free fall.

By conducting research on the ISS, scientists are able to design spacecraft that can safely transport humans through space, as well as perform experiments in an environment with microgravity, which can lead to breakthroughs in fields like chemistry and medicine.
