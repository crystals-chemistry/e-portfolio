A significant application of impulse in our daily lives is in cars. The airbags in cars are a safety measure designed with impulse, or change in momentum in mind. In most cases of accidents, the driver’s momentum carries them forward, towards the steering wheel. The use of an airbag, designed to inflate over a longer period of time, allowing for a smaller force to be exerted on the driver, decreasing the amount of momentum they have until they come to a full stop. Without the airbag, the driver would experience a large change in momentum over a shorter period of time, which would cause more damage.

Thelaw of conservation of momentumstates that when two or more objects collide, the collision does not change the total momentum of the two objects. Whatever momentum is lost by one object in the collision is gained by the other. The total momentum of the system is conserved.

Finally, there is a type of collision calledcompletely inelastic.This type causes the maximum amount of kinetic energy to be lost, as the objects stick together and move at the same velocity.

An example of a completely inelastic collision is two tennis balls colliding against each other in midair.
